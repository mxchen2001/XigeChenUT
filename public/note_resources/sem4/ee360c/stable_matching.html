Stable Matching Problem

Historically a heterosexual, monogmous marrage

  • Match a set of men (husband, applicants) with a set of women (wives, positions) optimally

What is "optimal"?

  1. No mutually beneficial swaps
  2. Maximize happiness

Problem

  • Applicants in summer internships
  • Grad school
  • Residency at Med school

We want self-reinforcing process of matching based on performances

An instability occess

  1. Applicant AAA prefers Postion P′P'P′ better over current employer PPP
  2. Position PPP prefers Applicant A′A'A′ better over current Applicant AAA

Defining the 2 sets

Where there are nnn applicants and nnn positions

Set of all ordered paris A×PA \times PA×P of form (a,p)(a,p)(a,p) represents a found pair

  • A Matching, S, is a set of ordered pairs each from A×PA \times PA×P s.t each member of AAA and each member of PPP appears at most once
  • A Perfect Matching is where each member appears exactly once (only if cardinality matches)
  • Each applicant to rank all position (total ordering) aaa prefers ppp to p′p'p′
  • Postions also ranked

Set up Preference List

  1. Every a∈Aa \in Aa∈A ranks p∈Pp \in Pp∈P
  2. Every p∈Pp \in Pp∈P ranks a∈Aa \in Aa∈A
  3. An instability occurs when S contains (a,p)(a,p)(a,p) and (a′,p′)(a',p')(a′,p′) such that aaa prefers p′p'p′ and p′p'p′ prefers aaa (both ways)
  4. This is unstable because aaa and p′p'p′ trade and a′a'a′ and ppp are left unpaired

Gale-Shapely

2 while ∃a\exist a∃a who is availiable and hasn't offered to every p∈Pp \in Pp∈P
3 \quad do Choose sicha a postion aaa
4 \qquad Let ppp be the highest ranked in aaa's preference list to whom aaa has not yet made an offer
5 \qquad if ppp is free
6 \quad \qquad then (a,p)(a,p)(a,p) becomes linked
7 \qquad else ppp is currently linked to a′a'a′
8 \quad \qquad if ppp prefers a′a'a′ to aaa
9 \quad \qquad \quad then aaa remains free
10\quad \qquadelse www prefers aaa to a′a'a′

Axioms

  1. www remains linked from the point of the first offer
  2. The offers given to mmm get progressively better
  3. Sequence of offer offered gets worse
  4. Position Optimal

Termination base on number of offers

  • Terminates after n2n^2n2 iterations at most ∴O(n2)\therefore O(n^2)∴O(n2)

Proofs for Gale-Shapely

Proof for G-S algo terminates after n2n^2n2 iterations
Each interation consists of position making offer to applicant it has not previously offered to. Count the number of offers. The number of offers always increase by 1 for each iteration. the total number of offers is bounded by n2n^2n2 (nnn applicants and nnn postions). The loop terminates after n2n^2n2

Theorem: If a position is open at any point in the algorithm, then there muyst be an applicant to which that position has not yet been offered
Proof:
Suppose not, suppose that at some point, ppp is open. But ppp has already made offers to all applicant. Then all applicants must be matched because G-S is bounded by n2n^2n2. But this is a contradiction ∴\therefore∴ ppp must be matched

Theorem: The Set SSS returned at termination is a perfect matching
Proof:
Suppose not, suppose the algorithm terminates with a open position. However, this position must have offered to every applicant. If the position made an offer to every applicant, it must be matched to at least 1. ∴\therefore∴ this contradicts the previous theorem.

Theorem: The SetSSS of matches returned by the G-S algo is a stable matching
Proof:

  1. Suppose not, suppose ∃(a,p)∈S\exist (a,p) \in S∃(a,p)∈S and (a′,p′)∈S(a', p') \in S(a′,p′)∈S s.t. aaa prefers p′p'p′ to ppp and p′p'p′ prefers aaa to a′a'a′.
  2. During the algo p′p'p′ offer to a′a'a′ most recently
  3. There are 2 cases
  • Case 1: p′p'p′ offered to aaa previously
    • Then aaa would have drop ppp for p′p'p′. Since that is not the case, this is a Contradiction
  • Case 2: p′p'p′ has not offered to aaa previously
    • Then p′p'p′ prefers a′a'a′ over aaa (Contradiction)
  1. ∴\therefore∴ G-S is stable

Data Structures

  1. Queue of availible positions
  2. 2 arrays of length n
    • position-match index by position
    • applicant-match index by applicant
    • invariant (a,p)
      • pos_match[p] = a
      • app_match[a] = p
  3. one or more array for progress
    • count[p] = # offers p has made
    • count[p] + 1 = ranking of next offer

Matching Process

  1. Dequeue an open positions, p. Make offer to the applicant who ranks at count[p] + 1 in p's preference list
  2. The applicant accepts if (1) app_match[a] < lower on app_pref[a] (2) app_match[a] == NULL
  3. if the applicant accepts, any dropped position is unpaired and goes back into the queue
  4. if the applicant declines p goes back into the queue
  5. count[p] is incremented

Representing Preference lists

  1. For position - ordered list, most to least preferred
  2. For applicant
    • map from "key" (position #) to the rank
    • "inverted" pref list

Valid match - position ppp is a valid match for applicant aaa if ∃\exist∃ some stable matching in which aaa is matched with ppp s.t. (a,p)(a,p)(a,p)

Optimality

  • Position optimal - every position received best valid match
  • Applicant optimal - every applicant received best valid match

Claims

  1. All executions of Gale-Shapley (as written above) yield a position-optimal assignment, which is stable.
  2. All executions of Gale Shapely (as written above) yields an applicant pessimal assignment.

Proof for Claim 1

  1. Suppose not, suppose G-S ends with some position ppp that does not have it best valid match
  2. ppp must have made an offer to its best valid match
  3. Consider the first time that any position ppp is rejected by some valid applicant aaa
  4. aaa movies to some other preferable postion p′p'p′
  5. p′p'p′ is preferred over ppp on aaa's pref list
  6. Since aaa is valid with ppp, ∃\exist∃ a S′S'S′ s.t (a,p)(a,p)(a,p) and (a′,p′)(a',p')(a′,p′) where a≠a′a \neq a'a=a′
  7. But since the rejection that ppp experience is the first executed rejection of SSS, p′p'p′ connot have been rejected by aaa
  8. Since p′p'p′ offers in order of pref p′p'p′ prefers aaa tp a′a'a′
  9. But (a,p′)∉S′(a,p') \notin S'(a,p′)∈/S′, ∴\therefore∴ contradiction and there is an instablility in S′S'S′
